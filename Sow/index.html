My Web Analysis

AWWWARDS

INTRODUCTION

Awwwards is a company who mainly organises web design competitions across Europe and the United States. Winners are decided by an industry professionals jury, which makes this award widely recognized in the web design world. Aside, it also provides educational courses of all different aspects of web design and all levels.

From these awards we are going to analyze one of its competing web projects called Grit Pictures.
Click here to see the webpage

STRUCTURE AND VISUAL DESIGN

Grit Pictures is a video production company. Its website is composed of a main page featuring the team, work, ethos and contact. Users can reach those sections by scrolling or by the menu located on the header. It's a fixed header where we can also find the business logo on the left side.
On almost every section we will find a round button that will take you to another page with all the information about that category. Each section works by blocks of information, mainly composed of images rather than text, which fits perfectly with the audiovisual company. It has very small margins on the right and left side, which creates a more chaotic visual aspect. Moreover, it has a very dark aesthetic full of black and white stickers and pictures.

INTERACTION

Is a vertical webpage. While scrolling, we will find some movement on the objects. We can differentiate a few categories from these moving objects along the web.
On one hand, we have the clapperboard that says “watch me” and links you to a video and the circular button that takes you to each section. Both of them are making little jumps in order to catch viewers attention and know they are clickable.

On the other hand we have the images along the homepage which are continuously changing in order to allow viewers to look at a lot of images from one sanction without needing to scroll. All of them change from black and white to color images while hovering.

Besides, it also has more moving objects along the webpage. Some elements do zooming, others turn around or change color. For example, one of the most interesting parts of scrolling is the section of “play our showreel”. While scrolling, few eye illustrations appear from both sides and if you continue scrolling, those eyes turn up in order to guide you again to the video. This is a way to encourage visitors to watch that video.

Finally, all the background is full of movement, which helps the webpage to not get flat and gives a sense of perspective. The pointer is also part of this interaction, as it is in charge of moving a large part of the background. Although it doesn’t perfectly work when changing it from one section to another, it has some visual problems.

USABILITY

Grit Pictures website is used almost like a portfolio of the company. It immediately shows the whole aesthetic of their business without watching their videos. The page doesn’t feel very clean at the beginning. However, once you understand their purpose and how the different sections work, it’s much easier than what you expected.
The website is really complete and you can find all the information you need about the company. Moreover, in case of needing more details, it also includes their contact email and some links to their social media accounts.
Even though the page isn’t too large, the speed isn’t really fast. There are a lot of elements and it’s not really clear which elements are clickable and which ones are not. This is not a negative issue, but rather likely the intention behind the website’s creation.

SPEED

1,8s

RESPONSIVENES

YES

EFFICIENCY

87

CONCLUSION

As a conclusion, this webpage used to show a video business has a really specific style that follows the brand aesthetic. It has a really simple structure, however the use of images and stickers make it look more complex.

In my opinion, the page looks really cool, but I had to spend quite a lot of time looking at how to enter each section. Most of the elements have some kind of movement, so they don't allow the button to stand out. Having found those key elements, we can see the rest of the page is really complete and clear.

WEBBY AWARDS

INTRODUCTION

Webby Awards are one of the most prestigious international recognitions of excellence on the internet. They have been presented by the International Academy of Digital Arts and Sciences (IADAS) since 1996. They have various categories, including Websites & Mobile Sites; Video & Film; Advertising, Media & PR; Apps, Software & Immersive; Social & Games; Podcast; Creators and the latest inclusion, AI.

From this competition we are going to analyze one of its participants' web projects called Limbo.
Click here to see the webpage

STRUCTURE AND VISUAL DESIGN

Limbo is a branding company. Its website is composed of a homepage, where it exposes some messages of empathy with the viewer, in order to show their solution later on. On the homepage we can also find some images that link us directly to their work and a footer with some contact information as well.
Everything is framed with a simple line that makes a square. On the top right of that frame we can find a menu icon with different sections: works; about; services; join us and citi. Most of this webpage is made with text, rather than images, which makes this web way too heavy for a branding business.

Limbo is composed of three main colors: blue, white and pink. This is a really powerful and outstanding combination for the brand. Along the page, they usually use white for the background, blue for text and pink for the illustrations and icons. However, the footer and heading have a blue background with pink letters, presumably to differentiate them from the main page.

INTERACTION

It is a vertical webpage. This web keeps a very simple visual structure, however, it has very interesting movements on it. The first movement we can see is on the logo. While scrolling, the logo creates a kind of circumference, which gives the same idea as their isotype (a rounded door with stairs). Stairs, arches or circles can be seen all over the page and most of them are interactive objects. For example, the footer has the shape of the stairs and when you scroll, the stairs move upwards.

Moreover, we can also find some illustrations that can be moved with the mouse pointer. They follow the main aesthetic of the website and some of them play with the isotype shapes as well. These are interactive elements added in order to counteract the text.

Another interaction is made each time you change a page. For example, if you change from the “homepage” to the “about” page, an animation with the door shape is made in order to introduce you to the new page. This also helps users to know exactly when they have changed and avoid confusion.

Finally, one of the most attractive designs for users is the mouse cursor. Instead of the traditional arrow, they created a hand pointer making the finger-pointing symbol, while also creating the letter “L” from limbo. Furthermore, it has a really interesting animation when you click. The hand snaps its fingers, which makes a wave that can move the interactive illustrations around it. Nevertheless, the cursor can sometimes be difficult to see with the white background due to its light colors.

USABILITY

Limbo serves as an informative webpage. It explains everything about the company and is very comprehensive. However, as a business that works greeting visual identities, limbo feels too explicit. They explain everything in detail, but users can just see how the company works in the work section. The rest of the page is full of text and doesn’t really show how this brand works.

The web’s structure is really clear and easy to understand, which creates a very direct and quick interaction to find what the viewer wants to see.

SPEED

1,0s

RESPONSIVENES

YES

EFFICIENCY

ERROR

CONCLUSION

To conclude, Limbo has a really nice aesthetic that perfectly matches their brand. Their use of javascript is really good and makes the page more interesting. However, it doesn’t catch all viewers' attention because of their large explanations. In my opinion, they should include more visual objects and reduce text. Moreover, they could also include some buttons for people interested in learning more about the company, instead of having everything on their web.

FWA

INTRODUCTION

The FWA is an award website that shows the most advanced innovations in digital design and development. It has an international reach, with judges from over 35 countries. They honor projects created by individuals; agencies and brands as well. They have different award categories: websites; apps; physical installations; VR and AR projects.

From this site we are going to analyze a webpage done for the 18th anniversary of the Portuguese digital studio Bürocratik. The website is called 18 years of Büro. Click here to see the webpage

STRUCTURE AND VISUAL DESIGN

18 years of Büro is a really minimalist webpage. It shows the story of Bürocratik through time. Its appearance looks like a timeline. It doesn’t have a menu, although a small index at the beginning with which you can go to a specific part of the page. Additionally, in the webpage also appear a little manu once you move forward. They divide the menu by four main chapters that are divided by years; they also include their social media and a link to their original page.

The aesthetic is mainly in black and white, however most of the images are with color and some illustrations have a bit of color too.

Its structure is based on time. So they first explain the beginnings of the company until 2024. It is a very long webpage full of information, dedicated to the Bürocratik team or for people really interested in it. Standard users are probably not going to read all the site, but maybe watch something specific of their interest.

INTERACTION

It is a horizontal webpage, however their responsive formats aren’t. 18 years of Büro has a really simple aesthetic, but still has some interesting interactions all over the page. The first page we see and what we can call some kind of homepage has two main interactions: in one hand we have the background that moves up or down with our pointer; and in the other hand we have the hand that points the “Go” button, which can be moved and makes a comment if you make a flip with it.

Furthermore, the main interaction that will take us through the entire website will be a line with a dot at its end, referring to a timeline. This object will be showing users the years they are talking about. It also moves up or downwards depending on the cursor.

Another movement we can see throughout the page is with some images. In some parts , while scrolling starts appearing with the pointer trajectory. It’s interesting because this makes you stop and stay longer trying to watch those images that flash by quite fast.

They also include some 3D elements with some interactions while clicking. Moreover, the pointer changes depending on the interaction. For example, for clickable objects, it changes to a hand; while for a video, it changes to two eyes to indicate “watch”.

USABILITY

This webpage is not an ordinary page. It is used as a commemorative page, which means that it doesn't really have any commercial purpose, but rather expresses gratefulness and perhaps serves an informative function.
Those interested in the company will directly go to the Bürocratik official website, instead of this one. However, this webpage is well structured and has a lot of visual objects, which makes it quicker and easier to watch.

SPEED

4,7s

RESPONSIVENES

YES

EFFICIENCY

87

CONCLUSION

To conclude, this webpage is really interesting regarding its structure and different purpose. It’s really well done for all devices, although small screens lose the horizontal essence of the timeline. Maybe it could be interesting to continue with the horizontal webpage by turning around the display.
It’s a quite heavy website, however it doesn't have any important business information. Also their use of images helps a lot to keep it eye-catching.

CSSDA

INTRODUCTION

CSS Design Awards is a design and development award platform that showcases digital freelance designers, studios and agencies. It's a very big company with international recognition. They have two types of judging systems: one with professional judges and another one with public votes.
It gives awards for each month and also a winner of the year.

From these honors we are going to analyze one of its participants' web projects called Seth Lukin.
Click here to see the webpage

STRUCTURE AND VISUAL DESIGN

Seth Lukin is a web design studio with a really specific appearance. Their webpage immediately sends you to the classic video game world. Most of the web is made with that pixel aesthetic. Furthermore, the color palette is really simple, based on black, white and grays, which make images stand out more.
It is a really visual website, divided in different sections. At the beginning of the homepage they briefly explain who they are and what they do. Then, they immediately show their work, divided in a few categories: UX/UI; web; motion; visual and experimental. They introduce you to each work with very big images that appear while scrolling. You can click on them and will take you to the explanation of that project. However, they don’t have any sticky menu, so each time you go back you need to scroll down again. After this they have a very visual list with all the things they do and a footer with their contact.

INTERACTION

It is a vertical webpage. Javascript is used all over the page, which makes contrast with the simplicity of its colors and illustrations.

We can find the first interaction on the website cover, where the background moves with the pointer. A boy appears jumping like the typical videogames characters, which already gives users an idea of the whole web aesthetic. If you click on that character, it will take you to a little game.

Moreover, the arrow has a significant role on the web. Not just because of its pixelated aesthetic, but also because it changes its appearance depending on the object. It helps users to understand when something is clickable or which objects are videos. Really useful for objects like the videogame from the beginning.

On the header we can find their mail and linkeding information instead of the typical menu. This produces an emphasis on getting in touch with them. It’s a fixed header with no background. This means that information needs to change color in order to stay visible in all parts. In this case, the information always has the opposite color of the background, no matter if many different colors are in the composition.This sometimes makes it difficult to read, although this is not a big problem, as we can read it in all the pages.

Finally, another important aspect of this page is that objects start appearing while scrolling, but aren't until the middle of the viewpost when you can fully see them. This forces you to scroll until seeing the whole object.

USABILITY

Seth Lukin is a webpage used as an introduction to this company work, probably as a kind of portfolio. The main objective is to show their works and find new clients.
As mentioned before, it is a really visual webpage, however, there is some missed information. They show their work and style in a very easy way, but don't provide any information about them, who they are, where they are from…
Moreover, the footer is composed of two main buttons. One for starting a project and the other to write an email, where the second one doesn’t work. In a computer it is not a problem because on the header it’s included their mail. However on a phone, the header only shows their linkeding information, which makes users unable to contact them by email. The responsive version for smaller devices doesn’t completely work. Some information is hidden by other objects

SPEED

14,1s

RESPONSIVENES

BADLY

EFFICIENCY

74

CONCLUSION

To conclude, this webpage has a very good and easy design in order to watch the company work and its aesthetic. It immediately involves you in their own world and invites viewers to see it. However, they need to improve the information about them, as it’s important for a client to know who they are going to work with, aside from their work. Additionally, nowadays almost everyone searches things from their phones, so it’s important to fix the responsiveness of the site.
